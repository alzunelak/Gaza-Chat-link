Offline Peer-to-Peer Messaging for Crisis Zones

GazaChat is a peer-to-peer offline messaging app for use in crisis zones with no internet access.

Problem

In crisis zones, internet and cellular networks are often shut down. People are left unable to communicate with loved ones, aid workers, or communities. There is an urgent need for secure, offline messaging that works without central servers.

Solutions

GazaChat solves these challenges using peer-to-peer WebRTC connections, QR-code-based handshakes, and offline-first storage via IndexedDB. All communication is encrypted, and the app runs as a PWA on mobile or desktop without requiring central servers.

Vision & Features

Vision: Empower communities in crisis to stay connected without needing the internet. Build a global, decentralized communication safety net.


Key Features:

  • Peer-to-peer encrypted messaging
  • QR code connection setup
  • Offline support with local storage
  • Progressive Web App (PWA) functionality
